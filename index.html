Anger Management Therapists

Job Description

Anger management therapists play a crucial role in helping individuals recognize, comprehend, and effectively manage their emotions without causing harm or disrespect to those around them. As trained professionals, they offer assistance and support to clients dealing with anger issues, assisting them in developing healthy coping mechanisms and communication skills. Anger management therapists use counseling and therapy strategies to assist people reach an agreement peaceful decisions about problems and helps them work on communication skills. These professionals make possible life-changing journeys toward mental stability.

Schools

To become an anger management therapist, you'll need to pursue higher education in fields like psychology and counseling. Typically you have to earn a bachelor's degree followed by a master's degree which can take around six to seven years in total. You may also need to complete additional training or certification specific to anger management therapy to enhance qualifications.

Here are some well-known schools offering relevant programs you can explore:

  • New York University (NYU)
  • University of California, Berkeley
  • Carnegie Mellon University

Salary/Hours

The average salary for anger management therapists ranges from $70,000 to $110,000 per year, depending on experience, location, and the type of setting they work in. The highest paid therapists can earn upwards of $100,000 annually.

Hours can vary, but typically include both daytime and evening appointments. Many therapists in this field have flexible schedules and may work weekends or holidays to accommodate client needs. On average, therapists work around 40 hours per week.
